Victoria Falls Fact

Victoria Falls is approximately 1.7 kilometers wide and 108 meters high, making it the world's largest sheet of falling water.

The Natural Wonder

Victoria Falls stands as one of Earth's most spectacular sights, spanning 1.7km wide and plunging 108m into the Zambezi Gorge. Known locally as "Mosi-oa-Tunya" (The Smoke That Thunders), this UNESCO World Heritage site creates a thunderous roar and a spray visible from miles away, forming magnificent rainbows across the landscape between Zambia and Zimbabwe.

Perfect Timing

Experience the falls at their most powerful from February to May when the Zambezi River swells from seasonal rains, creating an overwhelming curtain of water. For clearer views with less mist, visit between June and August. The dry season (September to November) reveals the dramatic cliff face and geological formations in striking detail.

Navigation Guide

Welcome to your immersive Victoria Falls experience! Here's how to make the most of your virtual tour:

  • Look around: Click and drag with your mouse, or swipe on touchscreens to change your view in any direction.
  • Zoom: Use your mouse wheel or pinch gestures to zoom in and out of specific areas.
  • Move to new locations: Click on the circular hotspots throughout the tour to move to different viewpoints around Victoria Falls.
  • Information points: Look for "i" icons that reveal interesting facts about what you're seeing.
  • Full screen: For the most immersive experience, use the "Immersive View" button to enter full screen mode.

Throughout your journey, you'll discover breathtaking viewpoints showcasing different aspects of this natural wonder. Enjoy exploring!
